Results: One important point we notice is that Random Forest performs best. This is due to its characteristic of using bootstrapping sampling. Multinomial Logistic Regression performed the worst. One important mention (though not shown here) is the decision tree we got from comparing good, average and bad wine instead of comparing quality 5, 6, 7 produces good visuals that penalize the minority (many nodes are minority, even though the minority counts is 60 times smaller than the majority counts). In addition, in the confusion matrix of our ANN algorithm, wine with quality 6 is usually misclassified as wine quality 5 and wine quality 7.
